

# 🎨 Dashboard Design — Valorant Tier 2 → Tier 1 Scouting

## 📌 Design Philosophy

This dashboard is designed as a **decision-making interface**, not a visual report.

Every element follows one principle:

> **Clarity over aesthetics. Decision over exploration.**

The design guides the user through a structured analytical flow:
**What is happening → Why it is happening → What to do**

---

## 🧠 Design Objectives

The design aims to:

- Enable **understanding in under 10 seconds**
- Support a **single core decision**: promotion vs non-promotion
- Avoid cognitive overload
- Ensure every visual has a **clear analytical purpose**
- Provide a **guided narrative** instead of free exploration

---

## 🏗️ Dashboard Structure

The dashboard follows a **3-layer architecture for decision-making**:

### 🟢 1. Executive Overview (What is happening)
- High-level KPIs
- Player distribution vs Tier 1 threshold
- Immediate understanding of the competitive landscape

👉 Design goal:
**Instant situational awareness**

---

### 🔵 2. Diagnosis (Why it is happening)
- Performance comparison vs Tier 1 benchmark
- Efficiency and reliability analysis
- Identification of structural performance gaps

👉 Design goal:
**Explain root causes of the problem**

---

### 🔴 3. Action (What should be done)
- Player evaluation table
- Model-based ranking
- Segmentation into actionable clusters

👉 Design goal:
**Drive a clear and defensible decision**

---

## 🎯 Visual Hierarchy

A strict hierarchy ensures fast comprehension:

### Primary Elements
- KPIs (large, centered, high contrast)
- Titles with clear semantic meaning
- Key charts (scatter, comparison)

### Secondary Elements
- Supporting charts
- Tooltips
- Segmentation labels

### Tertiary Elements
- Filters
- Interaction controls

👉 Users should always see:
1. The result  
2. The explanation  
3. The detail  

---

## 📊 Visual Selection Principles

Only essential visuals are used:

| Visual Type | Purpose |
|------------|--------|
| KPI Cards | Show core metrics |
| Scatter Plot | Detect relationships (Impact vs Readiness / Reliability) |
| Bar Chart | Ranking and comparison |
| Table | Final decision layer |

### ❌ Avoided
- Pie charts (low analytical value)
- Decorative visuals
- Redundant charts
- “PowerPoint-style” layouts

---

## 🎨 Color System

A minimal and functional color system is applied:

- **Background:** Dark (#111823 / #1a2330)
- **Primary Accent:** Red (#ff4654)
- **Positive Signals:** Green
- **Negative Signals:** Red
- **Neutral Elements:** Grey tones

👉 Purpose:
- Maximize contrast
- Focus attention on decisions, not decoration
- Align with Valorant visual identity (subtle thematic consistency)

---

## 🧩 Interaction Design

### Advanced Filtering
- Implemented as an **overlay side panel**
- Avoids reducing canvas space
- Activated via bookmarks

👉 Benefit:
Cleaner layout + controlled exploration

---

### Sync Slicers
- Shared filters across pages

👉 Benefit:
Consistent context throughout the analytical flow

---

### Dynamic Tooltips
- Provide contextual insights
- Compact design (~240px width)
- Replace the need for additional visuals

👉 Benefit:
More information without increasing visual clutter

---

### Filter Indicator

- Visual indicator showing active filters

👉 Benefit:
Prevents misinterpretation of filtered data

---

## 🧠 Cognitive Load Management

The design intentionally limits complexity:

- Maximum 5–6 visuals per page
- Clear spacing and alignment grid
- No competing focal points
- One message per visual

👉 Result:
Users can understand the dashboard without guidance or training

---

## 📐 Layout & Spacing

- Grid-based alignment
- Consistent margins
- Strong separation between sections
- Content grouped by analytical purpose

👉 Principle:
**Structure creates clarity**

---

## ⚙️ UX Decisions

### 1. No free exploration
The dashboard guides the user step-by-step instead of allowing random navigation.

👉 Reason:
Focused decision-making > open exploration

---

### 2. No redundant KPIs
Each KPI answers a specific question.

👉 Reason:
Avoid metric overload

---

### 3. Decision-first design
The final page (Action) is the most important.

👉 Reason:
The goal is not insight — it is decision

---

## 🚨 Common Design Mistakes Avoided

- ❌ Too many visuals per page  
- ❌ Lack of hierarchy  
- ❌ KPIs without context  
- ❌ Decorative dashboards  
- ❌ Inconsistent filtering  
- ❌ No clear decision output  

---

This dashboard achieves:

✅ Fast comprehension

✅ Decision-oriented experience  
✅ Professional UX standard
